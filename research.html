Navigation

We don't see space, we feel it instead. This was a consequence of the experiments of John O'Keefe and Jonathan Dostrovsky in 1971, when they found place cells in a rat's hippocampus, far from the visual cortex. Those cells fired each time when the rat was at certain places in the experimental box no matter what was the direction of movement. Their discovery inspired several new experiments on how we navigate in space.

I'm interested in how we can address new questions on this topic using virtual reality.
